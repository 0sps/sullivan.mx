## The verdict

Between April 2024 and April 2025, I was stalked for eleven months. Over 30 rotating fake phone numbers, constant harassment texts, and eventually someone physically vandalizing my car.

Gavin Grier was found liable for stalking, harassment, and vandalism by the Franklin County Municipal Court. He was ordered to pay fines and court fees. He did not appeal.

*(You can verify this yourself: go to [https://www.fcmcclerk.com/case/view](https://www.fcmcclerk.com/case/view) and search "Gavin Grier.")*

I've sat on this for almost a year, because unfortunately it keeps happening to other people. I have heard multiple examples of this individual hurting people around me in Ohio, so I feel obligated to share my story and make it public.

Everything here is from my perspective, and I can back all of it up. To protect third parties, all personal information besides Gavin's name has been redacted. I'll note where screenshots are being added; if you want to verify anything before they're up, email me at questions(at)sullivan(dot)mx.

## The pattern

The stalking was not constant. When I was talking to Gavin, things were quiet. When I pulled away or rejected him, it got bad.

The timeline makes this impossible to ignore.

---

### How we met (January – April 2024)

I met Gavin on Grindr in January 2024. I was wrapping up my last year at Ohio State in Columbus. He had just moved to Ohio from Seattle to attend flight school in Sidney.

We talked for a few weeks and eventually went on a real date on March 5th, Lyft to a bar called Lincoln Social. He was enthusiastic after that, texting to make plans, following up when I didn't respond quickly. I was in my most demanding semester of school and told him I'd probably have more time after I graduated in May.

Around April 4th I reached out and invited him to my fraternity formal that weekend. Short notice, plans that kept changing. I ended up canceling the next morning because it was too hard to coordinate that quickly. I stopped texting him after that.

That's when the stalker showed up.

---

### April 2024

The first messages came in right as I went quiet with Gavin. These messages consisted of homophobic slurs and generally aggressive texts.

What jumped out immediately was the slang they would use. The numbers kept spelling things "fkn" and "tmmr." I noticed those spellings specifically because Gavin used them in his own texts to me. It's not common shorthand.

By mid-April I was trying to figure out who was behind it. On April 15th, I got the number to call me via \*67, and because I had TrapCall set up on my phone, the actual number behind the restricted call was revealed. It belonged to a guy in Columbus I had briefly talked to and ghosted the previous year.

Later, another spoofed number traced back to someone in Seattle. In a text, that person said "his cousin" put him up to it. Gavin is from Seattle.

My read: Gavin had recruited people from his life, including family, to make calls and send texts on his behalf to avoid direct tracing.

---

### May – August 2024

In early May, Gavin and I started messaging on Snapchat again.

Right on cue, the harassment stopped. Last message from the stalker: April 17th.

We were friendly again through May. In June he came into town; we went to dinner and later joined up with my roommate Michael and a group of his friends. Gavin met Michael that night, and he met a woman named Kaylee who was part of the friend group and lived in Chicago. After that night, Gavin and Kaylee started texting regularly.

A few weeks later he told me he wanted something serious. I was honest with him: I didn't feel a romantic connection and wasn't interested in pursuing that. I suggested keeping things casual. He said no.

The last real conversation we had that summer was in July. I mentioned I was helping my parents move before a family trip to Europe. He said, "You can't just find some friends to replace them?"

I remember staring at my phone. Replace my parents, on a family trip they were paying for. I told him exactly what I thought of that. He left me on read. We didn't talk for months.

---

### September – October 2024

In September, someone messaged me on Grindr. Called himself "River." Said he was moving to Ohio in December for flight school.

Something was off from the start. His photos had old Grindr watermarks, the kind the app stopped using years ago. He texted back at all hours, instantly. When I ran his photos through a reverse image search, the only result was an adult film link. No social media, no college photos, nothing that a real person who exists would have.

I kept talking to him anyway. We texted for a few weeks. Eventually he asked about my history with guys.

I vented about Gavin. I called him an asshole, said he was a narcissist, said I thought he was a dick. The usual stuff you say when you're frustrated with someone.

Within one day of that conversation, "River" completely changed. He called me "super stuck up" and said he was done talking to me. Then he told me he had tracked down Gavin's number through a flight school schedule and texted him, sending along screenshots of everything I'd said.

I found this hard to believe on multiple levels. Flight school staff numbers aren't listed anywhere publicly. And this person, who supposedly had no connection to Gavin, suddenly cared enough to go find him and forward him my private venting.

Over the next few weeks, the stalker kept coming back specifically to bring up the word "narcissist." Not the other things I said. That specific word. He kept coming back to that one specifically, in a way that made me think it had actually hit something.

I eventually texted Gavin directly to ask if someone had contacted him. He said yes, the stalker had reached out. What he didn't do: express any surprise about how a random stalker had somehow gotten his unlisted work number. He just sort of shrugged it off. Told me the number was on the flight school's reservation website. It isn't.

### November 2024 – January 2025

At some point the stalker actually called me. I answered. There was no person on the line, just a prerecorded robotic voice reading a threatening message.

*[ Insert audio: robot voice call ]*

I got chills. This wasn't someone being annoying. This was someone who didn't want me to recognize them.

The texts kept coming through October and November. The angle had shifted: I had ruined things with Gavin, I was the problem, I was "stuck up" and unlovable. I'm not going to pretend it didn't work. I lost a lot of confidence during that period. I started to wonder if maybe I really had been unfair to Gavin.

So in mid-November I reached back out to him. Texted him on Snapchat, apologized, said I felt bad about how we'd left things.

He responded. The stalker immediately went quiet.

We texted through December and made plans. I went over in January, we talked, I spent the night. We went to a concert on January 28th. I drove us there in my car, a Kia sedan I'd bought the previous August. He stayed over again that night.

Nothing happened either time, and the next morning I texted him directly: I didn't know where my head was, and nothing had changed romantically for me.

### Late January – April 2025

The texts restarted immediately after I told Gavin I wasn't interested. This time they went to my Google Voice number too, not just my regular phone.

Then the stalker told me to go look at my car.

I went to the parking garage and checked from a distance, looking at another car nearby before slowly peering over at mine. It was vandalized.

I had traded in my old red Jeep in August 2025, after Gavin and I had stopped hanging out. Almost nobody knew I had a new car. Gavin had just ridden in it four days earlier.

After that, the harassment expanded. Michael started getting texts too. The numbers claimed to know exactly where Michael was at different points throughout the day. Some numbers posed as coworkers of mine, others as insurance agents. One spoofed call came from an iPhone we were able to trace to a Snapchat account connected to one of Gavin's associates.

---

### February – May 2025

By February, Michael and I had a solid theory and were trying to confirm it.

The location tracking texts were the clearest tell. Michael shared his Snap Map with friends, and Gavin was on his friends list. Once Michael turned off location sharing, the texts about where he was throughout the day stopped immediately.

We also started feeding fake information to Kaylee, casually, nothing dramatic. Michael mentioned to her that he was moving out because he hated living with me after all the stalking stress. Within days, the stalker texted us with that exact information, framed as an insult.

I filed suit in May 2025. The day I filed, the stalker texted me to let me know he had seen my boyfriend and me kissing in the park.

Gavin moved back to Washington after learning about the lawsuit. People in his circle told me he left specifically to avoid being served. It didn't work. The courts reviewed the evidence and found him liable.
